Biography

I'm a passionate creative, model, and recent college graduate with a heart for art, fashion, and inspiring others. I’m the proud winner of Miss The Next African Queen in Ireland 2024–2025, a journey that transformed me from a shy girl into a confident young woman.

Now, I use my platform to empower others through fashion modeling and art-based workshops where I help people express who they truly are. My mission is simple: to show others — especially young women — that they can be anything they dream of, no matter where they start.

Education

I graduated with a degree in Visual Merchandising from TU Dublin, where I honed my eye for design, storytelling, and customer experience. My studies gave me a strong foundation in creative direction, space planning, branding, and visual storytelling — all essential tools that now support my work in fashion, modeling, and artistic expression. One of my biggest takeaways from college wasn’t just what I created, but how I grew. It challenged me to think bigger, trust my creative instincts, and develop the confidence to present my ideas boldly. My time at TU Dublin was a key milestone in shaping both my personal and professional journey.


I also hold a qualification in Fashion Design from Crumlin College, where my love for fashion truly took shape. This course gave me hands-on experience in garment construction, fabric selection, sketching, and creating original designs from concept to runway. It taught me not only technical skills, but also the discipline, patience, and vision needed to bring ideas to life. Studying fashion design allowed me to express myself freely and explore how style can be a powerful form of identity and storytelling. It was a foundational step in my journey as a creative, helping me blend artistry with purpose.
